### Page Banner:

**Subtitle:** The People's Own Party of Odisha

---

### Sub-Section: The Problem with Odisha Today

Odisha is rich in natural resources — forests, rivers, minerals, coast — yet remains one of India's most underdeveloped states. According to various government and non-government reports, Odisha lags behind other states in nearly every key indicator:

- **Poverty:** A disproportionately high percentage of Odisha's population lives below the poverty line
- **Child & Maternal Health:** Infant mortality and maternal mortality rates remain among the highest in India
- **Migration:** Millions of Odias are forced to migrate to other states for daily wage work
- **Unemployment:** Especially high among youth, women, and tribal communities
- **Illiteracy:** Still significantly higher than the national average

Since the state's formation in 1973, despite multiple governments making promises, no fundamental structural change has occurred.

---

### Sub-Section: Why Existing Parties Have Failed

Odisha's existing political parties are trapped in old patterns:
- Led by elite upper-caste groups and dynastic families
- Caste-based vote-bank politics with no real inclusion
- Governed for personal benefit rather than public service
- Failed to develop science, technology, and skilled industry
- Allowed Odisha's resources — mines, forests, water — to be exploited by outside corporations without benefit to local people
- Rampant corruption, administrative failure, and political instability
- Drug and alcohol trade growing unchecked — causing social destruction, family violence, and community breakdown

The existing regional party has ruled for 9+ years without any fundamental economic change for the masses. "Poverty-creating" schemes spend public money without reducing poverty.

---

### Sub-Section: Who Is ABD?

ABD — **Amal Vikas Dal**, formally known as **Odisha Bahujan Samya** — is a new political force formed by and for the people of Odisha.

- **Founded by:** Social activists, community workers, and youth who believe in genuine change
- **Covering:** All 30 districts of Odisha
- **Flag:** Blue, White, and Red — bearing the Odisha map and Ashoka Chakra
- **Party Colour:** Blue (White, Red combination)

ABD is not just a political party — it is a social movement committed to changing the system.

---

### Sub-Section: Our Commitment (Pratibadhata)

ABD commits to achieving its constitutional and humanitarian goals, and to providing every citizen with the right to livelihood, equality, and dignity. Our goal is not just development of individuals, but overall, holistic development of all.

**Odisha is a state of:**
- Multiple languages and cultures
- Diverse religions and beliefs
- Numerous social communities

All of these communities deserve equal and proportional representation in governance, administration, business, education, judiciary, and literature.

## SECTION 9: MANIFESTO PAGE — Full Content

### Page Banner:
**Title:** Our Manifesto
**Subtitle:** 22 Goals for a New Odisha

---

### Introduction:
ABD stands for a fundamental and democratic political transformation of Odisha. We believe meaningful change will not come without political power. Politics is a tool for social transformation. Informed and organised citizens can change the governance system of the country and state.

---

### The 22 Goals:

**1.** Equal opportunities for all people of Odisha — through democratic, secular, and socially just governance. Work for social, economic, political, and cultural change.

**2.** Ensure adequate and assertive representation and leadership for the historically marginalized 15%+ tribal (Adivasi) population.

**3.** Proportional representation for all backward classes (OBC, ST, SC, Religious Minorities) in every field.

**4.** Maximum attention to the rights of Scheduled Tribes and Castes — especially regarding renewable resources and natural heritage.

**5.** Ensure representation of powerful Odia community groups in all areas and regions.

**6.** Sensitise toward religious minority communities' feelings of insecurity and ensure their adequate representation in all areas.

**7.** Secure women's leadership across all social classes — no field should have a glass ceiling for women.

**8.** Prioritise youth leadership across all social classes in every sector.

**9.** Full utilisation of the state's natural resources — land, water, minerals, forest, coast, and human resources. Facilitate state, national, and foreign investment opportunities.

**10.** Create investment opportunities for Odisha's government employees, teachers, businesspeople, and entrepreneurs through "Odisha Business Bank." Significant amendments needed in central laws regarding Odisha's mineral wealth and natural resources.

**11.** Provide all young and elderly people equal access to all resources; take appropriate measures for Adivasi (indigenous) Scheduled Tribe youth for all sectors (including government departments and businesses).

**12.** Particularly ensure maximum use of local customs for Odisha's original Adivasi communities — Sabar, Bonda, Bhuiya, Lodha, Pauri Bhumija — and provide leadership to their youth and young people.

**13.** Ensure those from socially disadvantaged and educationally backward classes in government leadership roles, with proportional representation for women, minority religious groups, and small communities in education, employment, and other sectors.

**14.** Eliminate fake Scheduled Caste, fake Scheduled Tribe, and other backward class impersonation from OBC lists. Provide Smart Caste Cards to all genuine backward communities.

**15.** Social equality — end caste-based discrimination and caste-based rituals. Equal respect and opportunity for all religions without promoting any particular religion.

**16.** No religion-based obligations or prayers at schools, offices, banks, and public places. Suppress Baba, Mata, Guru, Guni (faith-based) healers and non-scientific beliefs. Instead develop scientific, rational, and evidence-based thinking.

**17.** Promote inter-community and inter-religious harmony in Odisha. No social discrimination and social boycott in the name of any religious or cultural practice.

**18.** Ensure that to overcome disrespect and discrimination in all areas, the state guarantees all classes' participation through resources.

**19.** SC, ST, OBC, and Religious Minority communities — provide safety, security, and investment support for their economic development in Odisha.

**20.** Strong measures to stop all forms of injustice, corruption, nepotism, exploitation, partisanship, and oppression — through law, judiciary, and administration with highest priority.

**21.** Promote responsibility, accountability, compassion, sensitivity, emotional intelligence, value-system, and service attitude in politics and public life.

**22.** Cultivate values of human dignity, hard work, discipline, solidarity, and patriotism as identity of Odia character. Promote Odisha's development while building a strong sense of Odia identity; work to create an ultimate Odia defence force.

## SECTION 10: PROGRAMS PAGE — Full Content

### Page Banner:
**Title:** Our Programs
**Subtitle:** Concrete Plans for Every Section of Society

---

### LAND

- Land is a person's identity and source of livelihood. Use panchayat-level systems, land reform, and pilgrimage-home schemes to provide land to the landless — giving priority to Adivasi, backward classes, SC/ST, and small minority groups.
- Facilitate smooth land reform procedures for the landless.
- Completely end forced land occupation in Scheduled Tribe areas.
- Take steps to make unproductive land productive.
- Quickly resolve land-title and ownership disputes.
- Rebuild abandoned villages as planned settlements.
- Protect and maintain ancestral or community forest land.
- Provide funds for building community structures where needed.
- Make all lands available for use for 1,000 years in the future.
- Build affordable housing in cities for low-income families. No slums in cities.

---

### AGRICULTURE

- Support all types of agricultural development including land reform, science and technology use in agriculture, improved farming and pasture to boost agricultural productivity. Prioritise water management, watershed conservation, small water storage, canal systems, water distribution, and flood control.
- Use contract farming to connect all Gram Panchayat-level farming lands and nearby markets via road.
- Establish **"Odisha Krushak Bank"** for farmers — for all agricultural transactions.
- Provide agricultural workers, share-croppers, and farmers a **monthly pension of ₹5,000**.
- Crop protection, distribution systems, and agricultural organisations to work in coordination with state government regulations.
- Provide financial and technical support to farmers based on climate resilience and production capacity.
- Protect land rights guaranteed under Land Reform laws.
- Give priority to mechanised farming in high agricultural yield areas.
- In low-yield areas: try to encourage, with additional incentives, crops like ginger, chilli, sesame and farm types.
- Focus on commercial, profitable oilseeds, dal crops — vegetables, fruit produce, and more.

---

### EDUCATION

- Ensure primary education quality. Promote skill development including IT (Information Technology) in rural areas.
- Establish quality skill development centers and IT training institutions in unelectrified areas; send teachers from good schools there. Set teacher standards.
- Make education medium and career-oriented.
- Ensure equal educational standards and professional training for girls and boys.
- Schools, offices, banks, and all public places must be completely free of any specific religious prayer or rituals.
- Build proper infrastructure — libraries and playgrounds — in schools and colleges; maintain existing public education infrastructure.
- Arrange special education provisions for underserved and disabled students.
- Ensure quality management education from local schools to university levels; build proper vocational skills from school level so youth can start earning earlier. Particularly provide information about government and private sector career opportunities.
- Teach Odisha's own languages — Sambalpuri, Koraputia, Halbi, Kurma, and other languages — in schools. These language communities' right to mother-tongue education must be protected.
- Ensure equal and fair salary, payment, and career opportunity for both government and private sector teachers.
- Build at least 2 schools per Panchayat and proper government schools in tribal areas. Ensure quality education there.
- Work to implement the Right to Education Act for Odisha's citizens.
- No religious activities in educational institutions.

---

### HEALTH

- For epidemic disease prevention, prepare and maintain medical response infrastructure; train health workers.
- Establish proportionate numbers of doctors and paramedical staff in every Panchayat.
- Ensure treatment within timely timeframe; treat this as a non-negotiable and urgent priority.
- Establish primary health centers in every Gram Panchayat; through these, ensure regular health examinations reaching every citizen — especially mothers, children, elderly, and pregnant women.
- Completely ban quack doctors/healers.
- Ensure every family / household has clean and safe drinking water.
- Declare alcohol, bhang, and other intoxicant sales illegal and banned.
- Fill all vacant health department positions.

---

### MINING & INDUSTRY

- Apply protection rules for local knowledge and tradition in the state and ensure 90% reservation in Odisha's stone, mine, and sea transport sectors for Odia youth.
- Form **"Odisha Mining Area Employment Board"** for planning Odisha's mining and tribal areas. Through this board, invest mine area and tribal area affected people's income in mining shares; before any mining starts, ensure mandatory settlement, environmental protection, and local employment priority. At mine and mining industry levels, employ local people with other skill levels.
- Give priority to Odias and tribal people in mining business contracting, transport, and other activities. Ensure district-level mining party, Kanji, and other party-appropriate investment locally managed by local people.
- Provide necessary skills training for producing products requiring new knowledge.
- Make survey and research work into crafts and cottage industries more accessible with greater skill connection support.
- Regulate small, medium-scale industry education, product market and business development with planning.
- Give more attention to Mason Training School, Carpentry Training Centre, Fish and Marine Food Processing Training School, Mining Skill Training institutes.

---

### WOMEN

- Ensure women's leadership representation at all levels of the organisation. 30 to 50% women representation in all areas and 30% youth representation ensured.
- Organise State and district-level working committees with state officials and all district representatives; include others as permanent members as needed.
- In the organisation, build separate departments for various problems and issues; give responsibility to General Secretary and Editors.
- The organisation / party should adopt untouchability, casteism, communalism, gender patriarchy and discrimination, divisive behaviours and activities as non-negotiable policy.
- Give party priority to individual freedom, self-respect, social honour, honesty, patriotism.
- Give maximum priority to individual change, ideals and values, party discipline.

---

### SOCIAL REFORM

- End untouchability and caste-based discrimination. Strictly enforce legal action against caste-based practices.
- Provide equal respect and opportunity to all religions. But not promote any particular religious faith.
- Schools, offices, banks, and public places must not be subject to any specific religion's prayer or rituals. Suppress Baba, Mata, Guru, Guni faith healers and non-scientific beliefs. Develop scientific, rational, evidence-based thinking.
- Promote inter-community / tribal / religious harmony in Odisha. No religious or cultural practice should result in social discrimination or social boycott.
